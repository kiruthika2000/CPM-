How I work

Three rules for AI analytics products.

Hypothesis first, feature second

I don't build AI features because AI is trendy. I formulate testable hypotheses from user research and data, design experiments to validate them, and only ship what evidence supports. 8 of 12 hypotheses made it to production. The 4 that didn't? Killed early. That discipline saved months of engineering time.

Adoption is the product

A 93% accurate ML model means nothing if frontline teams don't use it. I design adoption into the product: embedded training, role-specific views, product champions. Traditional mandatory training got 34% adoption. My approach got 78% in 8 weeks — without a single mandatory session.

Actionable beats accurate

Frontline teams don't need a dashboard with 50 metrics. They need 3-4 numbers and a clear next action. My 'next best action' engine doesn't just predict risk — it tells you what to do about it. Teams following the AI recommendation achieved 38% better outcomes than those choosing manually.
